# Hidden Canon: Mizukawa Wiki Mystery

This file is for the generator and solve screen. It should not be shown directly
in the game UI.

Mizukawa is a rural riverside town that became known for the 1998 disappearance
of a quiet high school student named Emi Kuroda. The public story blamed a flood,
an overwhelmed police station, and confused witness reports. The real answer is
that Emi found evidence that Shirohane Clinic had been running unauthorized
memory and sleep studies on local residents under cover of community health
screenings.

The Mizukawa Folklore Preservation Society helped conceal the clinic's work by
owning land and archive rooms behind the clinic. Several members believed the
experiments were connected to older local stories about "festival bells" that
could be heard before disasters. That folklore explanation was mostly a red
herring, but it was useful for intimidating witnesses and making the town sound
superstitious rather than criminal.

On the night Emi vanished, the river did rise, but the official weather report
was edited two days later to make the flooding seem more severe than it was.
Emi's last school essay mentioned hearing festival bells from the west ridge,
where there was no active shrine. She had visited Shirohane Clinic shortly before
disappearing and may have found a patient ledger hidden inside a weather-station
maintenance cabinet. The clinic closed one week later. The folklore society owned
the land behind the clinic, including the shed where police should have searched.

The truth: Emi was not swept away by the flood. She discovered the connection
between the clinic, the edited weather report, and the society's land records.
Someone moved her through the closed service road behind Shirohane Clinic during
the storm. Her final location is unknown, but the coverup was organized by clinic
staff and at least two folklore society officers.

Core clues:
- The weather report from the night of the disappearance was edited two days later.
- Shirohane Clinic closed one week after Emi Kuroda vanished.
- The folklore society owned the land behind the clinic.
- Emi's last school essay mentioned hearing festival bells from a place where there was no shrine.
